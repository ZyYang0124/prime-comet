  - block: collection
    content:
      title: Unlocking Evolutionary Histories 
      subtitle: Phylogeny, Adaptive Evolution & Biogeography
      text: |
        <p>Our team is dedicated to deciphering the evolutionary relationships (phylogenies) of arachnids to map the origins and diversification of life. By leveraging cutting-edge phylogenomics—such as ultraconserved elements (UCEs) and whole-genome sequencing (WGS)—we build robust evolutionary trees across taxonomic scales, from ancient orders to closely related species.</p>

        <p>Beyond phylogenetic reconstruction, we integrate a suite of approaches to deepen our understanding of adaptive evolution:</p>

        <ul class="mb-4">
          <li><strong>Ancestral state reconstruction</strong> to trace the evolution of key traits.</li>
          <li><strong>Temporal divergence analyses</strong> to pinpoint the emergence of evolutionary innovations.</li>
          <li><strong>Historical biogeography</strong> to reveal where lineages diversified.</li>
          <li><strong>Comparative genomics</strong> to elucidate the genetic basis of adaptation and diversification.</li>
          <li><strong>Comparative morphology </strong> to understand functional adaptations and the evolutionary origins of anatomical diversity.</li>
        </ul>

        <p>Together, these approaches illuminate how, when, and where key traits have shaped biodiversity throughout deep time, providing insights into the adaptive evolution of arachnid lineages.</p>

      title: Arachnid Symbioses 
      subtitle: Microbe-Host Coevolution
      text: |
        <p>We investigate how <b>diverse symbiotic bacteria</b> shape the evolutionary trajectories of arachnids. Combining <b>high-throughput symbiont genomics</b>, <b>host phylogenomics</b>, <b>functional strain screening</b>, and <b>experimental assays</b>, we:</p>

        <ul class="mb-4">
          <li><strong>Map global symbiont diversity</strong> across major arachnid lineages (e.g., spiders, harvestmen) to define core microbiomes and host-specialized associations.</li>
          <li><strong>Decipher functional mechanisms</strong> enabling symbiont-mediated adaptation —including nutrient provisioning, detoxification, thermal resilience, and defensive mutualisms.</li>
          <li><strong>Track coevolutionary dynamics </strong> to reveal how symbiont transmission modes (vertical/horizontal) and host evolutionary history jointly drive adaptive innovation.</li>
          <li><strong>Identify and validate key bacterial strains</strong> through in vitro/in vivo assays to quantify their contributions to host resource acquisition, metabolic efficiency, and stress survival.</li>
        </ul>
